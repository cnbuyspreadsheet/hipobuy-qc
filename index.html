The four-counter inspection flow

No fake product database here. Use these counters to decide what one more HipoBuy photo should prove before the item joins a parcel.

COUNTER 01

Identity

Check the item, colorway, size tag, label, box code, and accessories against the linked product page before judging quality.

COUNTER 02

Construction

Look for asymmetric panels, weak embroidery, warped hems, bad glue lines, rough print edges, and missing hardware.

COUNTER 03

Measurement

Ask for a tape shot when the fit risk is real. Shoes need insole proof; apparel needs chest, length, waist, or sleeve proof.

COUNTER 04

Release

Only release after defects, missing photos, packaging needs, and category-specific red flags have a clear answer.

Verdict board

Use a simple outcome instead of endless second guessing.

Ship

Photos match the listing, size proof is enough, defects are minor or expected, and parcel packaging has no special risk.

Retake

One missing angle would change the decision: blurred label, hidden sole, no measurement, covered flaw, or warehouse lighting confusion.

Replace / cancel

Wrong item, obvious damage, bad pair matching, major sizing conflict, missing key accessory, or seller promise not matching the photo.

Retake ticket wall

Short requests get better warehouse photos. Copy one line, replace the bracket, and avoid a vague essay.

Please retake a clear photo of [area] in natural/bright light.
Please place a tape measure on [length/insole/chest/waist].
Please show both sides of [shoe/label/accessory] in one photo.
Please confirm whether the mark on [area] is dirt, reflection, or damage.

Lazy rule

Ask for one proof at a time. If the next photo cannot change ship/retake/cancel, skip it and move on.
